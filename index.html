About Me

My name is Burak Gunduz, and I am a first-generation university scholar reading Aerospace Engineering at Queen's University Belfast. My coding journey started whilst completing a summer research internship at the University of Oxford. I was taught Python as part of a data science course before starting my research project in the Department of Materials. From that point onwards, I taught myself HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, C++, and Ruby. I love solving complex problems and learning about new technology.

In my free time, I like to write and publish articles on Medium about engineering, technology, productivity, and entrepreneurship.

Electrical and Electronic Engineering Project

Language: C++

The objective was to design a microcontroller unit application capable of being turned ON and OFF by a pushbutton switch. Furthermore, the LDR sensor in the circuit had to detect ambient light by an analog input signal. Then using that information, the system was able to control the LED brightness using PWM signal in the full range. I coded the system using C++ on Mbed. I received a first-class grade of 95% on this project.
